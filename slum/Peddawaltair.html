Peddawaltair

Pedda Waltair is a slum area located in the city of Visakhapatnam in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. Pedda Waltair has a population of around 15,000 people, most of whom are daily wage laborers and belong to lower socio-economic groups.One of the key challenges faced by slum dwellers in Pedda Waltair is inadequate access to basic amenities such as clean water, sanitation, electricity, and healthcare. Many of the settlements lack proper sanitation facilities, which can lead to a range of health problems, including the spread of water-borne diseases. Slum dwellers may also face difficulties accessing affordable healthcare services, which can further exacerbate health problems and limit opportunities for economic and social mobility.

Major Problems

  • Slums are often characterized by high population densities and overcrowded living conditions. This can lead to a lack of privacy and a greater risk of the spread of diseases, particularly in the absence of proper sanitation facilities.
  • Poor housing conditions: Slum dwellers may live in substandard housing conditions, with limited access to proper ventilation, lighting, and insulation. This can impact their comfort and health, particularly in extreme weather conditions.
