Coramandal

Coramandal Slum is an informal settlement located in the Greater Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (GVMC) area in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is one of the oldest slum areas in Visakhapatnam and is characterized by densely packed makeshift houses made of temporary materials such as tin sheets, plastic sheets, and tarpaulin.

Major Problems

  • Poverty and Unemployment: Poverty and unemployment are pervasive issues in Coramandal Slum. Many residents work as daily wage laborers or in low-paying jobs, struggling to make ends meet. Limited job opportunities and lack of access to education and skill development further hinder their economic prospects.
  • Overcrowding: The residents of slums often live in cramped and overcrowded conditions, which can lead to social and health problems. Adarsh Nagar slum may also suffer from a lack of adequate space for its residents to carry out their daily activities
  • Lack of Infrastructure: Coramandal Slum often lacks basic infrastructure such as paved roads, street lighting, and proper drainage systems. This can lead to difficulties in transportation, increased crime rates, and poor living conditions.
